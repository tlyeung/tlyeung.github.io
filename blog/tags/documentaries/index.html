Documentaries July 2021

July 8, 2021 · One min read

Please vote for me#

Most of the people do not have a clue of what China is like. They consider China as another superpower like USA, but it isn’t that simple. This is a documentary for those people to learn more about China. It was about a class monitor election in a primary school in Wuhan in China, where the origin of COVID outbreak was as well. In the intro of the documentary we can see, the Chinese pupils had no understand of democracy or voting, they however had strong believe in patriotism. During the election period, those little boys and girls showed strong competitiveness to try to get votes from others, but they displayed no commitment in serving the class. They were simply fighting for the glory and power of the monitor role. In the film, we see bribing, physical threats, cheatings, lies after lies, all of the things that should not happen in a ten year old classroom happened. This is the real Chinese society, which has very limited (if not nothing at all) moral values and conscience.

Documentaries June 2021

June 22, 2021 · 3 min read

The pharmacist#

I finished a four-episode Netflix documentary series, called "The pharmacist", on 1st June. It was an exception series. Dan Schneider was like a boss, what a character. He was meticulous, intelligent, relentless and you can tell he had a lot of faith. He never gave up, never stop. He was a true hero. What is the right thing to do? I often ask myself. Now I can tell, we need to be a man like Dan Schneider. Highly recommended.

Dormir à tout prix#

I watched a documentary "Dormir à tout prix" on RTHK TV on 10th June. It was a very informative documentary that was about sleep. People nowadays do not sleep as good as people in the past, why? One of the problem is that LED has become very common in lighting, screens, monitors while it has similar wavelengths as sunlight. Exercise and eating habits are also essential to our sleep quality. Even the sleep quality of dogs have been affected by our change of life styles in recent decades. Sleeping occupies a significant amount of our life, but it remains one of the most mysterious thing about human. I have another question about sleep - is there any other members in animal world sleep face up, like human?

Black Holes | The Edge of All We Know#

I watched a documentary "Black Holes | The Edge of All We Know" on Netflix on 14th June. It was however nothing much about the science of black holes. It was more about how scientists work together to try to complete a difficult task. That was capture the black hole. Despite its lack of science knowledge in the documentary, it showed how scientists work together. When there are people, there are politics. The project in the documentary involves hundreds of scientists from so many countries, and there was so much funding behind them. We can see how those intelligent people cautiously trust each other and work with a democratic and scientific mindset. That is how our world should be like.

I am not alone#

I watched a fabulous documentary called "I am not alone" on 22th June. It was about the great democratic movement in Armenia in 2018. What a fairy tale, I could feel what Armenian had felt then, as we have had similar democratic movement one year after theirs in Hong Kong. I felt a sense of déjà vu when I watched this documentary. I was more than happy to know that they have earned democracy and by civil disobedience movement. The former prime minister, no matter what he had done before, was right to step down in the right time. I wish there will be no more totalitarian regimes in the world.

Documentaries May 2021

May 26, 2021 · 3 min read

The Immortals#

I watched a French documentary, called "The Immortals", on RTHK TV on 13th May. It was about some animals that have powerful recover ability, or simply do not age, or can survive in the most extreme environment. Do you know lobster, a common seafood, is one of the animals that never age? Human is probably the biggest threat to those old giant lobsters, and so to many other animals as well.

Le génie des arbres#

I watched a French documentary on RTHK TV on 17th May, it was called "Le génie des arbres". The French title means "The tree genius". Of course it was about trees. Trees are amazing creatures, they have senses like us. We know the fruits from the trees, we know how to make rubber or oil from trees, they are much more sophisticated than those. Trees do not have brains and do not move much, but in certain ways, they are quite similar to animals. Even scientists are still just starting to understand them.

Secrets in our DNA#

I watched a documentary on RTHK TV on 18th May, it was called "Secrets in our DNA". Today in the era of information, DNA has become one of the most important data. We can know many things with one's DNA and it is almost impossible to change. It is our privacy, and probably our top secret. But unlike other information, such as our social ids, or even the pictures of our faces, our DNA is predictable. Our DNA is inherited from our parents. It has become a trend that people provide their important data to some companies, that are not much restricted from the governments, to try to get something out of those DNA. For example, do they have secret siblings? Do they have a higher risk to have cancer? Where did they ancestors come from? One of the problems is that your lack of privacy concept may lead your relatives, even the one you have never met before, arrested. The documentary showed some murder cases, including the Golden State Killer, solved by the genetic correlations. But if any terrorists or totalitarian regimes control those databases and companies in the future, how can we prevent them, to trace the people or even their relatives, they do not like?

My Octopus Teacher#

I watched a documentary "My Octopus Teacher" on Netflix on 26th May. It was the Oscar winner of Best Documentary Feature this year. The structure of the documentary was very simple, just monologue and video clips. The story however was so authentic, very touching. It was a story about a man and an wild octopus. Octopuses are such beautiful creatures, they are intelligent and so different from any other animals we familiar with. We know the shapes of human, cats and dogs, horses and cows, even fishes. But Octopuses do not have a shape. They can be like rocks, carols, fishes and sands, everything you can find under the sea. They don't have shape, they can be solid or liquid. In the documentary, the octopus developed a strong bond with the man. The man was a big part of her very limited life. She lived fast and died young. You can see how she grew and learned and survived and laid eggs and died. She was curious and playful. I feel like I have become a friend with that octopus as well. It was a very nice documentary for all ages. I highly recommend it.
