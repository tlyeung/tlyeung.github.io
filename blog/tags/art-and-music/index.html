Concerts 2021

July 5, 2021 · One min read

It had been a while since the last time we can attend to concerts. And you never know when it will be the last time you do so.

RubberBand Ciao#

RubberBand is the best band in Hong Kong at this moment. They have already won almost all available awards in Hong Kong and of course have a lot of classics. Their concert in April was a very quality one. The name of the concert was "Ciao", does it mean it is their last show? Who knows.

ToNick HERE and NOW#

ToNick is an one of a kind band. Many of their songs were written in pure Cantonese, so it makes no sense if you can speak in Mandarin. If you want to listen to the most "Hong Kong" music, you should listen ToNick. Their concert in April had incredible atmosphere. I hope they can get more recognition, they are well deserved.

Botticelli Exhibition Review

March 9, 2021 · 3 min read

An exhibition of Florence's paintings in late 15th century, Early Renaissance, was held in Hong Kong Art Museum. 40 pieces from the Uffizi Galleries were displayed, including 10 from Sandro Botticelli, the most outstanding artist of that time. In fact he was so significant that the name of the exhibition was "The Hong Kong Jockey Club Series: Botticelli and His Times——Masterworks". One of his most famous works, "Adoration of the Magi", was shown in Hong Kong.

Art works are like sports, you have to watch them on site. Those impacts will be discounted severely if you watch them on monitors or TV. So it is very lucky for Hongkongers to enjoy these precious works in close range. It is obvious that the works from Botticelli outshine others' when I watched and compared them last week.

There were some other famous painters in the exhibition as well, such as Filippo Lippi and Filippino Lippi. But Botticelli's works were on different levels, although they have similar style. Botticelli was very good at putting details in his drawings. He was also good at the light, shadow and the transparent parts, you can find those touches in headdress, hair and clothing. Those skills made his work very stereoscopic, we were like "wearing a pair of 3D glasses", said my girlfriend.

The important patron, Lorenzo the Magnificent, also played a key role in the mature phase of the Golden Age of Florence. There would not have Botticelli if there had had no Lorenzo the Magnificent. Lorenzo is the one who creates that era. That era creates Botticelli. It is like wine vintages; good years creates good wines. If you live in a good year, you can achieve much more. For example, in 1927 the Fifth Solvay Conference, it was the best time of physicians. Botticelli was like Einstein in early Renaissance.

We are in a special time as well. We now have the Internet and new technologies. We can create more things to more people in a faster pace. But are we using what we have to create better artworks? Are we doing our best to solve real problems, such as "food waste and hunger at the same time in the world", but not wasting time in ideologies conflicts? We have better cinematic technologies, but we are not making timeless movies like the "Godfather", "Amadeus", "the Shawshank Redemption" etc.?

Are we in a better or worse time?
