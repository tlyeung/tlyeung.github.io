Movies July 2021

July 5, 2021 · One min read

Luca#

Luca was a comfortable cartoon movie to watch. If you love Italy, like me, then it will be a movie you don’t want to miss. Glad to see many classic Italian elements in this movie, sea and boats, Vaspa, football and cycling, pasta and gelato, etc. It was a good choice for family film.

The story was easy to understand. It was educative as well, able to taught a lesson to children (or frankly everyone), that we should not be afraid of someone or something that we do not understand.

I would give the cartoon a 8/10.

Movies June 2021

June 29, 2021 · 3 min read

Persian lessons#

I watched a movie "Persian lessons" on 5th June. It is a story about a jew pretend to be a Persian, to try to survive in a concentration camp during WWII. The plot was interesting but there were also some flaws. But, all in all, it is still a good movie to watch. The two main characters were outstanding. I give this movie a 7.5/10.

Quo Vadis, Aida?#

I watched a movie "Quo Vadis, Aida?" on 26th June. This was a powerful movie. I have heard of what had happened in Yugoslavia in 1990s, but it was my first time to see the massacre story in cinema. History repeats itself. I felt sorrow for the Bosnians but I felt more furious to the UN. It should take much of the responsibility of the tragedy because of their indifference and inability. The UN peacekeeping force were soft, weak, and useless, and so stupid to make wrong decisions after wrong decisions. The UN should be closed, it is a completely waste of money. What is the point of its existence if they could not save the people who had already sheltered in their base, and let them to be killed just next to their base? I think this is the same for all international organizations that involves so many nations, such as WHO, which should take the responsibility of the COVID outbreak for its slow reaction. When I watched the movie, I was thinking, what if there was a journalist in the crowd, or if the UN force can contact a journalist to bring this to the attention of the people from all around the world, people would know and maybe thousands of lives can be saved. That thought reminds me of Apple Daily, I feel much more unsecured to live in Hong Kong after its closure. I will rate this movie 8.5/10.

Hand Rolled Cigarette#

It was a gang crime drama which we have seldom seen in Hong Kong in recent years. Hong Kong had had a lot of gang crime movies in the past. But many consider they have been out of trend now. The young director of the movie was a notable. The file editing and cinematography were also excellent. I will give this movie a 7.5/10.

Cruella#

I have watch movie “Cruella” on 29th June. It was a high quality Disney movie, which had exceeded my expectation. The story was quite fresh, Emma Stone and Emma Thomson made it even better. The character Jasper was however vague, definitely not a lover, not quite a friend, not quite a follower, of Cruella/ Stella, his story line was wasted. I will rate the movie 8/10.

Movies May 2021

May 28, 2021 · 2 min read

Promising Young Woman#

I watched a movie "Promising Young Woman" on 4th May. It was the 2021 Oscar winner of the best original screenplay. But I don't think it deserved such honour. It seems average to me. Maybe there were fewer movies last year due to COVID. Some screens reminded me of another movie this year, "I care a lot", and I found them very unrealistic that a woman could scare off angry men that easily. According to my experience, embarrassed men tend to use violence even more. My girlfriend did not like the revenge idea, I however disagreed with her. Revenge is a must when it is needed. Revenge is one of the common themes in many good movies, even among those all time greats such as the Godfather. I give this film a 6/10.

Mank#

I watched (but did not finish) the movie "Mank" on Netflix on 28th May. It was disappointing. I do not oppose black and white movies, some of them are my favourites, e.g. Casablanca. But the production of "Mank" was, I don't know how to say it better, bad. It looks like a youtube video, or the work by a freshman in movie college. If there is a black and white movie, I expect the cinematography should be like "Schindler's List", or "The Artist". "Mank" was nowhere near that standard. I have never watched a worse movie by David Fincher than this.
