I watched a movie "[Promising Young Woman](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9620292/)" on 4th May. It was the 2021 Oscar winner of the best original screenplay. But I don't think it deserved such honour. It seems average to me. Maybe there were fewer movies last year due to COVID.  Some screens reminded me of another movie this year, "[I care a lot](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt09893250/)", and I found them very unrealistic that a woman could scare off angry men that easily. According to my experience, embarrassed men tend to use violence even more. My girlfriend did not like the revenge idea, I however disagreed with her. Revenge is a must when it is needed. Revenge is one of the common themes in many good movies, even among those all time greats such as the Godfather. I give this film a 6/10.

I watched (but did not finish) the movie "[Mank](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt10618286/)" on Netflix on 28th May. It was disappointing. I do not oppose black and white movies, some of them are my favourites, e.g. Casablanca. But the production of "Mank" was, I don't know how to say it better, bad. It looks like a youtube video, or the work by a freshman in movie college. If there is a black and white movie, I expect the cinematography should be like "Schindler's List", or "The Artist". "Mank" was nowhere near that standard. I have never watched a worse movie by David Fincher than this.
